# ethics
Key principles to consider
- integrity
- fair treatment
- confidentiality
- respect
- inclusion, diversity and belonging

# integrity
Integrity is based of
- honesty: If you make a mistake, acknowledge it right away rather than hiding it.
- transparency: Keep the team/supervisor informed about your progress
- accountability: follow through on commitments and deadlines. Ask for extra time/resources proactively, rather than the last minute

# fair treatment
Fair treatment ensures that all individuals feel valued, heard and supported. To promote a positive work environment where everyone can contribute to stay strong in decision-making and innovation.
Can be done through
- Respecting other's idea
- Been inclusive in teamwork
- Seeking/Providing constructive feedback

# confidentiality
This is a fundamental ethical and professional responsibility in the workplace, which ensures trust, between everyone.
It can be done by
- Handling sensitive data: Make sure only the correct people are allowed to access certain data
- Ask for permission: whenever you are unsure about what should be shared, go ask
- Protect digital info: Lock your device when stepping away from them.

# respect
Respect is essential for creating a healthy, inclusive and productive workspace.
Can be achieved through
- Appreciating diverse persepectives
- Avoiding negative behaviour
- Respecting boundaries and personal space

# inclusion, diversity and beonging
- Inclusion is necesary to create a supportive productive environment for everyone, especially for __students entering their first professional experiences__. 
- Diversity refers to recognition and appreciation of the differences among individuals.
- Belonging is one level above diversity, it's about making an environment where every employee feels valued, heard and involved.
