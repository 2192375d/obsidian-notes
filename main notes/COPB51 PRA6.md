# to take COPB52, I need
- Maintain your status (in Coop) by having a CGPA of 2.5+
- CGPA < 2.3 means removed from coop
- 2.3 <= CGPA < 2.5 means on probation from coop
- CGPA >= 2.5 means you are doing good

# Work term sequencing options
- There are 7 standard work term sequences, all available for CMS students
- ___something___

# eligibility requirements
You need to consider the followings
- coop subject POST (program in UTSC)
- valid work permit (international students need to apply for work permits in advance of their job search to avoid delays)
- GPA requirements
- additional course requirements based on programs
- elligibility assessment: coop office checks your eligibility every term, make sure you are always 2.5 CPGA above

# academic planning
Double check that you have met all the requirements from a booking with AA&CC.
Use Degree Explorer to double check as well

# Tips from WTEC
- Do not wait to apply, do it __ASAP__ (Some job closes earlier if they got enough people)
- Use the time (right now + the gap) to get more experiences
- Talk to COOP staffs to get to know how to do job searches

# strategic job application framework
- week 1 & 2: apply 20 or more jobs
- week 3 & 4: apply 20 or more jobs
- after that: apply 10 or more
You should expect around 40 or more jobs by the end of week 4 (46% of the job are available during this time period).
You should expect around 50 or more jobs by the end of week 12

# apply jobs carefully
Once you get an offer, you can only say no for "one" job in total. Make sure you only apply to the jobs you like.

# decide on the jobs
- Are you applying to coop exclusive jobs?
- Have you clear career goals with a long-term plan?
- Are you applying to job based on title or job duties?
- Are you ready to apply for jobs that are not competitive?
- Is an external job search an option?
